Astronomy studies objects and their phenomena in space. It is a natural science that uses mathematics, physics, and chemistry in order to explain their origin and evolution. There are many objects of interest. These include planets, moons, stars, nebulae, galaxies, and comets.
Our Solar System contains 9 planets. The star of our solar system is the sun. The 9 planets are pulled by gravity to our sun and circle it. The planet closest to the sun is Mercury. Mercury is followed by Venus, then Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptun and the finally Pluto. Actually Pluto is officially only a dwarf planet .
Our Galaxy, the Milkyway, is a part of a very very large system called the Universe. The universe is more than 10 billion years old. It was created through the "Big Bang". The scientists will tell you that it was started through a singularity and quickly expanded and is still expanding. When we look in any direction we can see objects up to 46 billion light years away. Nobody knows how big the universe really is. There are also theories now which talk that there are multiple universes. It's called M-Theory.
Our solar system of the Milkyway galaxy. It's a spiral galaxy. It is centered around a large black hole that is currently asleep. So far, astronomars have found more than 3200 other stars with planets orbiting them in our galaxy. Overall, our galaxy has more than 100 thousand million stars. Our solar system is nearly on the edge of our galaxy. Astromers will tell you, that we are located on the 4th spiral arm.
