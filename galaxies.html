A galaxy is composed of gas, dust, and billions of stars and their solar systems. This is all held together by gravity. Our galaxy is named Milkyway and we are almost at the edge of our galaxy. In the center of our galaxy resides a sleepy black hole. Astronomers classify galaxies into three major categories: elliptical, spiral and irregular.
Elliptical galaxies do not swirl. They have a rounded shape of an ellipse. Elliptical galaxies are made up of mostly old stars, and do not have much gas and dust. There is very little new star formation in these galaxies. The largest galaxies we can see are elliptical, but they also come in very small sizes.
Irregular galaxies have no particular shape. They are full of gas and dust which means that these galaxies have a lot of star formation going on within them. This can make them very bright. These galaxies are amongst the smallest ones.
A spiral galaxy has stars and gas clouds concentrated mainly in one or more spiral arms. Our galaxy is a spiral galaxy. The galaxy shown here is Andromeda our nearest neighbour.
